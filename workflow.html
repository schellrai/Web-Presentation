Medical Imaging

Every patient is unique, and so is their anatomy. Using CT or MRI scans, we reconstruct a three-dimensional model of the structures we want to study. This gives us a precise, personalized starting point that reflects the real geometry of the human body.

Digital Twin

With the anatomical model in hand, we build a virtual replica — a digital twin — that can simulate how the body behaves under different conditions. Just like a flight simulator trains pilots without leaving the ground, our digital twin lets us run experiments that would otherwise be impossible or unsafe. This way, we can explore and understand complex biological processes entirely on the computer.

Device Embedding

Once we have our virtual model, we can place medical devices into it and study how they interact with the body — all before anything happens in a real patient. This allows us to test different configurations, optimize designs, and anticipate potential issues ahead of time. Ultimately, the goal is to make treatments safer and more effective for each individual.
